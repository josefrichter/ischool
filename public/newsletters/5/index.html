Christmas Concert

A year passed and we were once again trying to figure out what to prepare for the Christmas concert. About a month before the concert the whole Junior School started to practise Christmas songs every afternoon (Must Be Santa, Jolly Old Saint Nicholas, What Child Is This, Jingle Bells and We Wish You a Merry Christmas), so all the children could learn the words of the songs by heart. Some of the children also learnt to accompany the songs on our classroom instruments such as bongo drums, djembe, glockenspiel, swirl xylophone, kazoo, cabasa, jingles and triangle. It was really encouraging and satisfying to see that the children were so enthusiastic about all the singing and playing. Their genuine enthusiasm kept me going.

“Someone gets lost” - how the idea of a play turned into a wonderful show

I am very happy and proud to announce that our very own year 4 and year 6 children did an amazing job, performing at the Christmas concert, their own play, “Someone gets lost”.

One Boring Day in Pompeii

As we were learning about the Romans and the eruption of Mount Vesuvius with MP2 IPC class just shortly before Christmas, we thought that we could write a play in which we could show to the parents as well as to other children what exactly we had learnt about them. And that could also be a nice and graceful Exit Point to the whole unit! So we sat down with the children and started to brainstorm our ideas. It took a few days till we came up with a final version of the script but the nerves were worth it! The children studied hard and learnt their lines every single day as the date of the concert was coming closer. And at the same time they created wonderful stage props out of the cardboard and painted them. They also participated in the costume-making process! Mentioning the costumes, I must send a big thank you to Mrs. Itaniemi who helped out with the curtains and Mrs. Ringger who sewed our Roman togas and stolas together. Without their help I would be absolutely desperate as I am really not a needle lover. But as you all can see in the photographs the children had a great laugh and learnt a lot of things all at once. All of them are stars!

Communication “Media Magic” IPC

The long word communication is definitely hard to pronounce for young children, but vital to learn more about. This IPC unit shall help them to navigate way through this often overwhelming, media-saturated world.

The Entry Point to Communication Unit brought Y1 and Y2 together. While children were enjoying their lunch, teachers were turning the ordinary classroom into a real cinema with curtains, comfy seats and popcorn! As soon as the tickets were sold, movies started. There were no dialogues in them. So when the light went on, we tried to think together what the films were about. Then each group got a chance to tell their stories without words to the audience using just magic objects – a ring, a stick or a hat.

Year I and 2 will devote the whole term learning about media and communication from different angles. And then the children will tell you much more about the film than “It was funny!”
